## What was learned

- The candidate self-join needs no direction-dedup: fixing `d.amount_minor < 0` and
  `c.amount_minor > 0` in the `WHERE` makes each unordered transfer surface exactly
  once, so canonical order is only needed for the *stored* decision, not the scan.
- `list[A] | list[B]` defeats mypy's element-type narrowing (invariance → `object`);
  a covariant `Sequence[A | B]` parameter fixes it cleanly since both records share
  the accessed fields.
- The project keeps SQL as plain literals (never f-strings) specifically to stay
  clear of bandit B608 — the confirmed-ids sub-check is spelled out as literal
  `NOT EXISTS` in each query rather than factored into a constant + f-string.

## What was deferred

- Near-amount / fee-tolerant matching (exact magnitude only); re-validating a
  confirmed pair after `reassign_account` (accepted benign — the two rows net to
  zero in any account); a pending-count badge; an undo-reject "show rejected" view.
  All recorded in the spec's Out of scope. No `docs/known-issues.md` entries created.
