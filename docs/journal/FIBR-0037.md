# FIBR-0037 — Branded app icon — closed 2026-07-09

Pulled forward from P13 (user brought an icon concept mid-session). **Kind:**
implement (asset + wiring). Unblocks FIBR-0015 (the platform builds bake in the icon).

## What changed

- **Design (with the user):** iterated from a filing-cabinet/safe "storage" concept
  to a **"spending by category" donut** — four bold coloured segments
  (green/blue/teal/orange) with a **gold coin** in the centre, on a dark navy tile.
  Chosen because it (a) says *categorise your finances* — the app's real job — not
  "store/lock away", and (b) survives shrinking: it stays legible as a coloured ring
  with a warm centre all the way down to **24px** (verified by downscaling each
  candidate and eyeballing 24/32/48px).
- **`assets/icon/finbreak.png`** — the single **1024²** master (the design source).
- **`scripts/make-icons.sh`** — a reproducible generator that derives the whole
  platform set from the master (single source of truth, no drift): Linux PNGs 16–512,
  a 7-size Windows `.ico`, the macOS `.iconset`, and the runtime
  `src/finbreak/ui/icons/app.png` (512).
- **`ui/icons.py`** — `app_icon()` returning the packaged raster (mirrors the
  existing `icon()` `Path(__file__).parent / "icons"` idiom, so it resolves from
  source **and** a frozen bundle).
- **`app.py`** — `QApplication.setWindowIcon(app_icon())` right after the app is
  created (before `window.show()`), so every window/dialog **and the OS taskbar
  entry** carry it.
- **`pyproject.toml`** — package-data glob extended `["ui/icons/*.svg"]` →
  `[..., "ui/icons/*.png"]` so `app.png` ships in a wheel/pip install. The
  PyInstaller `--add-data` is directory-level, so the PNG already travels into the
  frozen bundle.
- **`_selftest.py`** — `_check_icons` now renders `app_icon()` too, so
  `python -m finbreak --self-test` (and the clean-room AppImage build-smoke) fail if
  the raster doesn't travel (DoD #2).
- **Test** — `test_FIBR0037_app_icon_renders` (app_shell suite): the packaged
  `app.png` exists and renders a non-null 64px pixmap; plus a headless
  `setWindowIcon`→`windowIcon()` round-trip was run to confirm the exact `app.py`
  wiring.

## What was learned

- **Small-size legibility is the real test for an app icon.** The early
  filing-cabinet concepts looked great at 1024px but turned to mush at 32px (too many
  small elements). The donut's bold ring silhouette holds at 24px. Downscaling
  candidates and looking at 24/32/48px caught this before committing to a design.
- **Gold, not green, for the coin.** There's no such thing as a green coin; the warm
  gold also separates cleanly from the cool ring at tiny sizes (better contrast than
  the green centre it replaced).

## What was deferred

- **macOS `.icns`** — generated at mac build time from the committed `.iconset` via
  `iconutil`/`png2icns` (neither is on this box, and no macOS build exists yet). The
  `.iconset` (the portable input) is committed, so the FIBR-0015 mac build just runs
  one command.
- **The AppImage/`.desktop` desktop-entry icon** — still the build-smoke's PIL
  placeholder; real Linux/mac desktop packaging is **FIBR-0015** (ADR-0007), out of
  scope here. This item delivers the **in-app** window/taskbar icon.
- **No CI regenerate-and-diff of the icon set** (a drift guard) — deliberately not
  added: it would need ImageMagick as a CI dependency, which the project avoids. The
  master + committed generator is the single source; re-run `scripts/make-icons.sh`
  after changing the master. Noted as a known minor (indie-review LOW).
